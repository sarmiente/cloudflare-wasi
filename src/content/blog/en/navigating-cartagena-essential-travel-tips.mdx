<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold font-pattern">FAQs</h2>
<strong>Q: Is Cartagena safe for tourists?</strong> 
<p class="font-pattern text-lg">Cartagena is generally safe for tourists, but it's essential to stay vigilant. Stick to well-traveled areas, secure your belongings, and use reputable transportation options.</p>

<strong>Q: What is the best time to visit Cartagena?</strong> 
<p class="font-pattern text-lg">The best time to visit Cartagena is during the dry season from December to March. This ensures pleasant weather for exploring the city and enjoying outdoor activities.</p>

<strong>Q: Do I need to speak Spanish to navigate Cartagena?</strong> 
<p class="font-pattern text-lg">While many locals speak some English, learning basic Spanish phrases can enhance your experience and help you connect with the local culture.</p>

<strong>Q: Are credit cards widely accepted in Cartagena?</strong> 
<p class="font-pattern text-lg">Credit cards are commonly accepted in tourist areas and upscale establishments. However, it's advisable to carry some cash for smaller vendors and local markets.</p>

<strong>Q: What are the must-visit landmarks in Cartagena?</strong> 
<p class="font-pattern text-lg">Don't miss Castillo San Felipe, the Walled City, and the historic churches in the Old Town. These landmarks showcase Cartagena's rich history and architectural beauty.</p>

<strong>Q: How can I experience Cartagena's vibrant nightlife?</strong> 
<p class="font-pattern text-lg">To experience Cartagena's nightlife, explore salsa clubs in Getsemani, enjoy beachfront parties, and visit popular bars in the Old Town. The city comes alive after sunset.</p>
